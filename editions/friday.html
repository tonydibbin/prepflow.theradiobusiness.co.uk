04 — Newsbrief

Quick hits

  • 1
    Almost 70% of UK households now have a smart speaker — but the most common use is still 'asking what time it is'.
    Researchers say we under-use the devices we own. Setting timers, playing music and weather queries make up two thirds of all commands.
  • 2
    Brits now spend an average of 14 hours a week on streaming — more than cooking, cleaning and exercising combined.
    The biggest jump is in the over-55s, who used to be the holdouts for live telly. 'Second-screening' means we pay less attention than ever.
  • 3
    Pubs report a Tuesday boom — the new 'mid-week meet' is the fastest-growing dining occasion.
    Operators say WFH-ers like getting out the house mid-week without the weekend crowds. Tuesday wine sales are up almost 30%.
  • 4
    The average Brit checks their phone 159 times a day — but says it 'doesn't feel like that much'.
    Researchers using passive monitoring apps say we badly underestimate our own usage. Most people guess between 30 and 70 times.
  • 5
    Half of UK adults can't name a single member of the cabinet — but 80% can name three Love Island contestants.
    Political engagement researchers say it's not apathy so much as 'noise overload'. People remember faces from things they actively watch.
  • 6
    More than half of UK workers would take a 10% pay cut to never go into the office again — but only 17% of bosses say they'd offer one.
    The gap is growing again after forced return-to-office rules. HR experts say the tension shows up in resignation rates, particularly for under-35s.
  • 7
    Britain's most-stolen supermarket item in 2026 isn't meat or cheese — it's olive oil, with theft up 220% year on year.
    Prices have nearly doubled in two years. Some shops have fitted security tags to single bottles, others have moved premium brands behind the counter — like cigarettes.
