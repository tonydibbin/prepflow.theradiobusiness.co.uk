04 — Newsbrief

Quick hits

  • 1
    One in three of us has had a 'sleep divorce' — sleeping in a separate bed from our partner.
    Reasons: snoring (45%), different schedules (30%), restless legs, and one bizarre 12% who say their partner 'talks to the cat in their sleep'.
  • 2
    Holiday firms report a 40% spike in 'shoulder season' bookings — September and October sun trips are way up on last year.
    Travellers blame school-holiday flight prices, unreliable summer weather, and 'August feels too crowded these days'. Spain, Greece and Portugal top the wish list.
  • 3
    Three quarters of dog owners now talk to their dog more than they talk to their partner.
    Top topics: 'what we're doing later', 'what we just saw out the window' and a surprising amount of Bake Off commentary. 12% admit they've apologised to the dog after a row with the human.
  • 4
    The 'silver gamer' is the fastest-growing demographic in UK gaming — over-65s spent £180 million on games last year.
    Cosy life-sim titles like Stardew Valley and Animal Crossing dominate. Console makers are reportedly designing larger UI for the market.
  • 5
    Britain's most-stolen supermarket item in 2026 isn't meat or cheese — it's olive oil, with theft up 220% year on year.
    Prices have nearly doubled in two years. Some shops have fitted security tags to single bottles, others have moved premium brands behind the counter — like cigarettes.
  • 6
    Cash is making a small comeback — ATM withdrawals are up 14% on this time last year.
    Bank analysts credit budget-conscious households using cash to stay disciplined. The 'envelope method' is back trending on TikTok.
  • 7
    Brits spend an average of 38 minutes a day deciding what to watch — and 12 of those are spent scrolling Netflix.
    'Watch-paralysis' has its own subreddit. Streamers are quietly experimenting with 'shuffle' buttons that just play something.
