04 — Newsbrief

Quick hits

  • 1
    Pubs report a Tuesday boom — the new 'mid-week meet' is the fastest-growing dining occasion.
    Operators say WFH-ers like getting out the house mid-week without the weekend crowds. Tuesday wine sales are up almost 30%.
  • 2
    One in three of us has had a 'sleep divorce' — sleeping in a separate bed from our partner.
    Reasons: snoring (45%), different schedules (30%), restless legs, and one bizarre 12% who say their partner 'talks to the cat in their sleep'.
  • 3
    The average Brit checks their phone 159 times a day — but says it 'doesn't feel like that much'.
    Researchers using passive monitoring apps say we badly underestimate our own usage. Most people guess between 30 and 70 times.
  • 4
    62% of us have lied about reading a book to look smart in front of someone we fancy.
    Most-faked titles: 1984, Pride and Prejudice and (awkwardly) the Bible. One in five admit to faking a whole dinner-party conversation about a novel they'd only seen the cover of.
  • 5
    More than half of UK workers would take a 10% pay cut to never go into the office again — but only 17% of bosses say they'd offer one.
    The gap is growing again after forced return-to-office rules. HR experts say the tension shows up in resignation rates, particularly for under-35s.
  • 6
    Holiday firms report a 40% spike in 'shoulder season' bookings — September and October sun trips are way up on last year.
    Travellers blame school-holiday flight prices, unreliable summer weather, and 'August feels too crowded these days'. Spain, Greece and Portugal top the wish list.
  • 7
    Almost two thirds of us say we've cried at a podcast in the last year — grief, parenting and identity top the list of triggers.
    Researchers reckon it's the intimacy of audio that does it. The figure is even higher for under-30s, 71% of whom admit a recent podcast-induced tear.
