04 — Newsbrief light bites · pick & mix

Quick hits

  • 1
    The most-Googled DIY question in Britain this year: 'how to remove silicone'.
    Tradespeople say it's the rise of botched bathroom resealing jobs during lockdown that's still haunting people. YouTube tutorials on the topic have racked up 60 million views.
  • 2
    Loneliness is now considered a bigger health risk than smoking 15 cigarettes a day, according to NHS England.
    Social prescribing — GPs referring patients to community activities — is rolling out nationwide. Walking groups, book clubs and choirs are reportedly oversubscribed.
  • 3
    The fastest-growing hobby of 2026 is hand-embroidery — sales of needles and thread up 180% in a year.
    Crafters say it's the perfect 'phone detox' — slow, tactile, satisfying. TikTok is full of 20-somethings showing off custom denim jackets.
  • 4
    Three quarters of dog owners now talk to their dog more than they talk to their partner.
    Top topics: 'what we're doing later', 'what we just saw out the window' and a surprising amount of Bake Off commentary. 12% admit they've apologised to the dog after a row with the human.
  • 5
    Almost 70% of UK households now have a smart speaker — but the most common use is still 'asking what time it is'.
    Researchers say we under-use the devices we own. Setting timers, playing music and weather queries make up two thirds of all commands.
  • 6
    British baby names are getting shorter again — three- and four-letter names are dominating the registrar.
    Top picks this year: Noa, Ezra, Ada and Bo. Long, multi-syllable Victorian names like Theodora are sliding back down the chart.
  • 7
    Brits now spend an average of 14 hours a week on streaming — more than cooking, cleaning and exercising combined.
    The biggest jump is in the over-55s, who used to be the holdouts for live telly. 'Second-screening' means we pay less attention than ever.
